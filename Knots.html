Braiding and Knots

May, 2024

A braid can be formed by exchanging the endpoints of strings. Take for example a braid with i = 5 points. We can represent an exchange with the (i-1) number of operations, σ1, σ2, σ3, σ4 . σi exchanges the i point with the i+1 point. The inverse of an exchange is given by σ-11. If you wanted to exchange points 1 and 3, it would be equivalent to doing σ1 then σ2 then σ1 again. Similarly, all other swaps can be rewritten as a combination of σi's and σ-1i's.

A link is a can be formed from taking the closure of a braid. This is drawn by connecting opposite dots without without crossing them.
